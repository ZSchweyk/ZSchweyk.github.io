Self Balancing Seesaw with Ping Pong Ball

GitHub Repo

During my senior year of high school and between other larger projects, I put together the hardware and wrote the software to self-balance a ping pong ball on a seesaw (from lazer cut acrylic). As the video and pictures above show, I wired up an Arduino Uno to a LIDAR sensor to track the position of the ping pong ball, and a servo motor to control/adjust the pitch of the seesaw. Although this project was relatively simple, I found it to be very neat since it introduced me to controllers and PID loops, which only took around 150 lines of Arduino C/C++ code to implement.

During my first couple days of working on this project, I experimented a lot with many different ping and sonar sensors. Some sensors could not read as far as the length of the seesaw, while others were not accurate enough to approximate the position of the ball. After a while, I found the VL53L0X Lidar, which has an accuracy within 3% of the true readings for distances up to 2 meters. The last figure above shows this sensor attached to one end of the seesaw. I made use of the Adafruit_VL53L0X.h library to read the LIDAR's distance measurements, and Arduino's built-in Servo library. Once I got everyone wired up and verified I was able to succesfully communicate with the LIDAR and servo, I began learning about basic PID controllers to solve similar control problems like this. I tuned the PID constants Kp, Ki, and Kd, manually until the results were decent. Something interesting I found was that once the ping pong ball was centered and stationary, the servo would still make sharp angle adjustments to keep it centered. I fixed this by scaling my Kp, Ki, and Kd constants down when the ball was centered and relatively stationary, and re-scaling the constants back up when the ball wasn't centered and stationary.
